Discover Provo's Culinary Scene


Provo, nestled in the heart of Utah County, is a vibrant culinary destination boasting a population of approximately 117,335 people as of the 2020 United States Census.


Here's a glimpse into the demographics:


  • Population: 117,335
  • Median Age: 24.9 years
  • Median Household Income: $49,623
  • Racial Diversity:
    • 84.5% White
    • 15.1% Hispanic or Latino
    • 3.4% Asian
    • 1.2% African American
    • 0.9% Native American
  • Educational Background:
    • 95.1% High School Graduates
    • 42.6% Bachelor's Degree or Higher
    • 13.2% Master's Degree or Higher
    • 4.8% Professional Degree or Higher

With its diverse population and rich educational background, Provo offers a melting pot of culinary influences and talent, making it an ideal hub for food enthusiasts and entrepreneurs alike.


Explore Provo's Culinary Statistics


Get insights into the culinary landscape of Provo with these statistical data:


  • Number of Restaurants: 389
  • Number of Food Businesses: 562
  • Number of Culinary Schools: 3
  • Annual Revenue Generated by the Culinary Sector: $267 million

These statistics highlight the vibrant culinary ecosystem in Provo, showcasing the opportunities available for culinary enthusiasts and businesses in the area.



Discover Provo's Culinary Attractions


Explore the culinary attractions that make Provo a delightful destination for food enthusiasts:

  • Farmer's Market: Offering fresh, locally grown produce and artisanal food products
  • Food Festivals: Annual events celebrating the diversity of cuisine in Provo
  • Culinary Tours: Guided tours showcasing the best eateries and culinary landmarks in the city
  • Food Truck Roundups: Regular gatherings of food trucks serving a variety of delicious fare

These attractions contribute to Provo's vibrant culinary scene, providing visitors with memorable experiences and culinary adventures.
